Rebel with a pencil

What's it like to spend a morning studying the same music theory that mozard studied and then an afternoon experimenting with vocal tones most people might not even consider singing?

For 21-year-old Julia Meadows, that kind of delightfully unexpected scholarship was just what she expected when she came to thThe New School's Mannes School of Music, attracted by its world-class voice instruction and personal mentorship program.

"I knew The New School would put together projects that were not predictable," the voice major says.

Meadows also found support for her interests beyond the classical singing and opera that brought her to The New School. In a recent turning point - aided by her voice instructor - she let her passion for musical theatre shine through.
